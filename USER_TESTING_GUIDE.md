# User Testing Guide — LinkedIn Learning Skills Feature

## Overview
- **Feature:** Personalized Skills widget on LinkedIn home page + skill assessment + job-skill gap analysis + course recommendations
- **Prototype:** Interactive HTML prototype (8 screens)
- **Goal:** Validate whether personalized skill-job matching increases awareness of LinkedIn Learning and drives engagement
- **Duration:** 20-30 minutes per session

---

## Pre-Interview Setup

### Logistics
- Share the prototype URL or screen share
- Have this guide open for reference
- Use a note-taking tool (Google Docs, Notion, etc.)
- Record the session (with permission)

### Participant Criteria
- LinkedIn users (active in last 30 days)
- Mix of: job seekers, working professionals, career switchers
- Mix of: LinkedIn Learning users and non-users
- Target: 5-8 participants

---

## Interview Script

### 1. Introduction (2 min)

> "Thanks for joining! I'm testing a new feature concept for LinkedIn. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm interested in your honest reactions. I'll ask you to walk through a prototype and think aloud as you go. Can I record this session?"

### 2. Background Questions (3 min)

1. What's your current role / career situation?
2. How often do you use LinkedIn? What for mainly?
3. Have you ever used LinkedIn Learning? Why or why not?
4. When you think about career growth, how do you identify what skills to develop?
5. Have you ever taken an online course (Coursera, Udemy, etc.)? What motivated you?

### 3. First Impression — Home Screen (3 min)

> "Imagine you just opened LinkedIn. Take a look at this screen and tell me what you notice."

**Observe:** Do they notice the Skills widget in the left sidebar? The promo card in the feed? The new nav item?

**Questions:**
- What catches your eye first?
- Do you notice anything new or different on this page?
- What do you think the blue "Skills" widget on the left is about?
- On a scale of 1-5, how likely would you click on it?

### 4. Task 1: Explore Skills Dashboard (4 min)

> "Go ahead and click on the Skills widget or the nav bar item."

**Questions after they land on the dashboard:**
- What's your first reaction to this page?
- What information is most useful to you here?
- What does the "72% Avg Job Match" mean to you?
- Do the job matches seem relevant? Why or why not?
- What would you do next on this page?

### 5. Task 2: Take the Assessment (5 min)

> "Let's try taking the skill assessment. Go ahead and start it."

**Observe:** Do they understand the role selector? Do they engage with quiz questions? Any confusion?

**Questions:**
- How did the assessment intro make you feel? (Motivated? Skeptical? Neutral?)
- Were the questions relevant to your experience?
- Was the length appropriate? (Too short / just right / too long?)
- Did you feel the questions could accurately assess your skills?

### 6. Task 3: Review Results (3 min)

> "Take a look at your results."

**Questions:**
- Do these results feel accurate based on your self-assessment?
- Is the skill level categorization (Advanced/Intermediate/Beginner) helpful?
- How do you feel seeing the job match percentages?
- What would you want to do with this information?

### 7. Task 4: Gap Analysis (3 min)

> "Now check out the job-skill gap analysis."

**Questions:**
- Is this view helpful for understanding what you need to learn?
- Do the green (have) and red (need) skill tags make sense?
- Would this motivate you to take a course? Why or why not?
- How does this compare to how you currently find skill gaps?

### 8. Task 5: Course Recommendations (3 min)

> "Browse the recommended courses."

**Questions:**
- Do these recommendations feel personalized or generic?
- What information on the course card is most important to you?
- Does seeing "+15% job match" influence your interest in the course?
- Would you start a course from this page? Which one and why?

### 9. Task 6: Course Detail (2 min)

> "Click into a course to see more detail."

**Questions:**
- Is there enough information here to decide whether to take the course?
- How do you feel about the "Impact on Your Profile" section?
- What's missing that you'd want to see?

### 10. Wrap-Up Questions (5 min)

1. Overall, how would you describe this feature in your own words?
2. On a scale of 1-10, how useful is this feature to you? Why?
3. Would this make you more likely to use LinkedIn Learning? Why?
4. What was the most valuable part of the experience?
5. What was confusing or could be improved?
6. **Key validation question:** "If LinkedIn Learning was personalized for your jobs (with high match percentage) where you might have a skill gap, would you utilize LI Learning instead of online courses like Coursera?"
7. Would you recommend this feature to a colleague?
8. Any other thoughts or feedback?

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | How to Measure |
|--------|---------------|
| **Discoverability** | Did they notice the widget without prompting? (Y/N) |
| **Click-through** | Did they click the widget unprompted? (Y/N) |
| **Comprehension** | Could they explain the feature in their own words? (1-5) |
| **Perceived Value** | "How useful is this?" rating (1-10) |
| **Intent to Use** | "Would you use LinkedIn Learning because of this?" (1-10) |
| **Preference vs. Competitors** | Would they choose this over Coursera/Udemy? (Y/N + why) |
| **Task Completion** | Could they navigate the full flow without help? (Y/N) |
| **Engagement Signals** | Which screen did they spend most time on? |
| **NPS-style** | "Would you recommend this feature?" (1-10) |

## Synthesis Framework

After all interviews, organize findings into:

1. **Patterns** — What did 3+ participants agree on?
2. **Surprises** — What unexpected feedback emerged?
3. **Validations** — What hypotheses were confirmed?
4. **Concerns** — What risks or objections surfaced?
5. **Recommendations** — What to change in the next iteration?

### Success Criteria
- 70%+ of participants find the feature "useful" (7+ rating)
- 60%+ say they'd use LI Learning because of this feature
- 80%+ can navigate the full flow without assistance
- Average NPS of 7+
